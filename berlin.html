Berlin

Jul 13–19  ·  7 nights Hotel Adlon Kempinski  ·  Museum Island  ·  Kulturforum
Hey Wolfie — here are my top travel tips, museum details, and recommendations for your trip to Berlin. I've added a few important logistical updates — especially a major heads-up regarding the Pergamon Museum — so you can plan smoothly.
Museum Island
Pergamon Museum Das Panorama
Crucial warning: The main Pergamon Museum building is currently closed for massive, multi-year renovations. However, you can still see the spectacular Ishtar Gate and a stunning, giant 360-degree exhibition of the altar at The Pergamon Museum. Das Panorama — right across the canal from Museum Island.
Altes Museum
The primary home for ancient Greek and Roman art on Museum Island.
Neues Museum
World-class classical antiquities alongside its famous Egyptian collection — including the Nefertiti Bust.
Alte Nationalgalerie
Go here for the iconic Romantic landscapes of Caspar David Friedrich — including The Monk by the Sea.
Deutsches Historisches Museum
On Unter den Linden. Note: the grand permanent exhibition hall is under renovation, but the ultra-modern Pei Building right behind it hosts brilliant rotating temporary history exhibitions.
Kulturforum
Gemäldegalerie
The world-class Old Masters collection — where you will find Hans Holbein and Lucas Cranach the Elder.
Neue Nationalgalerie
A striking modernist glass building designed by Mies van der Rohe — houses 20th-century art, including the works of Otto Dix and the German Expressionists.
WWII & Cold War History
Bendlerblock
This historic building served as headquarters for the German Army resistance during WWII. In the courtyard you will find the Memorial to the German Resistance — the exact spot where Colonel Claus von Stauffenberg was executed after the failed July 20, 1944 plot to assassinate Hitler.
Plötzensee Prison Memorial
Located in northwestern Berlin — a deeply moving, solemn memorial dedicated to the thousands of political prisoners executed by the Nazi regime here, including many of the July 20th conspirators.
Landmarks
Kaiser Wilhelm Memorial Church
On the Kurfürstendamm. The bombed-out spire of the original church stands as a dramatic anti-war memorial, right next to a striking modern blue-glass chapel. The chapel regularly hosts stunning evening organ and chamber music concerts surrounded by intense blue stained glass.
Alexanderplatz
Setting of Alfred Döblin's Berlin Alexanderplatz — a short walk from Museum Island. Read a chapter there.
Theater am Schiffbauerdamm
Kurt Weill's Threepenny Opera — featuring "Mack the Knife" — premiered here in 1928. Still standing, still a working theater. Walk past it.
Brandenburg Gate
Right outside the Adlon — impossible to miss.
Classical Music & Opera
Berlin Philharmonic (Berliner Philharmoniker)
At their iconic hall in the Kulturforum. Look up the performance schedule — if you can catch them live, the music list below becomes a preview instead of homework.
Staatsoper Unter den Linden
Berlin's most historic opera house, on the famous boulevard.
Deutsche Oper
Bismarckstraße 35, Charlottenburg — one of Berlin's three major opera houses.
Komische Oper
Known for more accessible and innovative productions.
Local Food
Currywurst & Berliner Knacker
Grab a traditional Currywurst (sliced pork sausage topped with spiced curry ketchup) or a classic Berliner Knacker from a street kiosk.
Kreuzberg — Turkish & Döner
Berlin is famous for its Turkish diaspora. Head to Kreuzberg or Wedding for an authentic Döner Kebab — the modern street-food form was popularized right here in Berlin.
Spaghettieis
A classic German summer treat. Go to any traditional Italian-German Eisdiele and order this — vanilla ice cream pressed through a noodle machine to look like spaghetti, topped with strawberry "tomato" sauce and white chocolate "parmesan" flakes.
Day Trips
Potsdam — absolute must-do
Just a 30-minute ride on the S-Bahn (Line S7) from central Berlin. Rent a bicycle outside the station and spend the day exploring Frederick the Great's Sanssouci Palace, the New Palace, and Cecilienhof Palace — where the 1945 Potsdam Conference took place.
Dresden
High-speed EuroCity trains from Berlin Hauptbahnhof in just under 2 hours. The historic Altstadt is beautiful, and the completely reconstructed Frauenkirche — rebuilt from WWII ruins — is entirely worth the trip.
What to Read
Goes light to heavy — don't start with max deadlifts.
Franz Kafka
Start with the parables: "Before the Law" (600 words — read it tonight), "An Imperial Message," "A Little Fable." Then the longer stories if hooked. Schocken Complete Stories has everything.
Heinrich von Kleist — Michael Kohlhaas
Trans. Michael Hofmann (New Directions). 100 pages. A horse dealer wronged by a nobleman takes his case from the courts to open rebellion — the great German story about justice. Based on a true story.
Theodor Storm — The Rider on the White Horse and Selected Stories
Trans. James Wright (NYRB Classics). Dikes, the North Sea, ambition, a ghost horse. Perfect for a train ride. Great ghost story.
Rainer Maria Rilke — Letters to a Young Poet
Trans. Stephen Mitchell. Written to a man your age asking the questions you're asking. Read slowly.
Hans Fallada — Every Man Dies Alone
Melville House. Berlin under the Nazis, based on a real Gestapo case file — an ordinary working-class couple who dropped anti-Hitler postcards around the city until they were caught. Pairs with Bendlerblock and Plötzensee: this is what those memorials are about. See the film version below.
Thomas Mann — Buddenbrooks (if ambitious)
Trans. John E. Woods (Vintage). Four generations of a merchant family in decline. He wrote it at 25; it won him the Nobel.
Alfred Döblin — Berlin Alexanderplatz (if ambitious)
Trans. Michael Hofmann (NYRB Classics). The Berlin novel — and Alexanderplatz is a short walk from Museum Island. Read a chapter there.
Films
Wings of Desire (Wenders, 1987)
Angels over divided Berlin, shot on location — you'll recognize the streets.
Alone in Berlin (2016)
The Fallada adaptation — Gleeson and Thompson.
Valkyrie (2008)
The July 20 plot. Especially watch after visiting Bendlerblock. Great film about my greatest heroes.
Mephisto (Szabó, 1981)
An actor sells his soul to the Reich — Klaus Maria Brandauer is terrifying.
Das Boot (1981)
The director's cut if you have the stamina.
All Quiet on the Western Front (2022)
German-language version.
Music — a German canon starter kit
Where possible, listen to the Berlin Philharmonic recordings — Karajan especially. You'll be hearing the hometown band, and if you catch them live at the Philharmonie, this list becomes a preview instead of homework.
Bach — Mass in B minor
Karajan/BPO recorded it, though purists prefer period bands — try both.
Beethoven — Symphonies 3, 7, 9; "Emperor" Concerto
Karajan's 1963 Beethoven cycle with the BPO is the classic entry point.
Schubert — Schwanengesang
Fischer-Dieskau with Brendel.
Schumann — Kinderszenen
Wagner
Tannhäuser Overture and Pilgrims' Chorus; Siegfried's Funeral March from Götterdämmerung; Prelude and Liebestod from Tristan; Lohengrin Act I Prelude. Karajan/BPO's Wagner orchestral excerpts album covers nearly all of these on one disc.
Brahms — Symphony No. 3 (third movement especially), Violin Concerto, Piano Quartet No. 1 in G minor
Kurt Weill — "Mack the Knife" from The Threepenny Opera
Which premiered in 1928 at the Theater am Schiffbauerdamm — still standing, still a working theater. Walk past it. Also: Alabama Song covered by The Doors.
Present-day Berlin
Radio Citizen, Berlin Serengeti ("The Hop" feat. Bajka)  ·  Superpitcher, "Country Boy"  ·  Tess Parks & Anton Newcombe, "German Tangerine"
